CHAPTER 1: PRODUCT OVERVIEW
========================================================================

1.1 Description
The DX-H2S-4000 is a fixed-point, single-gas electrochemical detector designed for
continuous monitoring of hydrogen sulphide (H2S) in industrial environments. The unit
provides a 4-20 mA analogue output and Modbus RTU digital output for integration
with process control systems and safety PLCs.

Operating Range: 0–50 ppm H2S
Resolution: 0.1 ppm
Accuracy: ±5% of reading (at full calibration)
Response Time: T50 < 15 seconds, T90 < 30 seconds
Alarm Setpoints (factory default): Low = 1 ppm, High = 5 ppm, STEL = 5 ppm (15 min TWA)

1.2 Intended Applications
- Coke oven gas handling areas
- Blast furnace tuyere platforms and cast houses
- Sour water treatment facilities
- Underground and confined spaces
- Chemical storage areas

1.3 Principle of Operation
The electrochemical sensor cell oxidises H2S at a sensing electrode. The resulting
electrical current is proportional to H2S concentration. The cell is temperature-
and humidity-compensated via onboard sensors.

Sensor cell life: Typical 24–36 months under normal conditions.
Cell degradation is accelerated by: high humidity (>90% RH continuously), high H2S
exposure (>10 ppm continuous), temperature extremes (<-10°C or >50°C), and
exposure to inhibitory gases (acetylene, ethylene, high concentrations of CO).

========================================================================
CHAPTER 3: INSTALLATION AND COMMISSIONING
========================================================================

3.1 Location Selection
Sensor placement is critical for reliable detection. H2S is heavier than air
(specific gravity 1.19 vs air = 1.0) and will accumulate in low-lying areas and
depressions. Detectors should be mounted:
- 150–300 mm above floor level in enclosed spaces
- At breathing zone height (1.2–1.5 m) on platforms
- Near potential leak sources (valves, flanges, pump seals)
- No closer than 500 mm to air handling ducts or exhaust fans (dilution effect)

3.2 Commissioning Procedure
Step 1: Verify supply voltage: 24 VDC ±10% (or 110/240 VAC for AC model)
Step 2: Allow 30-minute warm-up before calibration
Step 3: Verify sensor reads 0 ppm in fresh air (zero calibration)
Step 4: Apply certified calibration gas (see Chapter 5)
Step 5: Verify correct alarm relay operation at Low and High setpoints
Step 6: Test Modbus/4-20 mA output to connected control system
Step 7: Complete commissioning record (Appendix B)

========================================================================
CHAPTER 4: FAULT DIAGNOSIS AND ERROR CODES
========================================================================

4.1 Fault Display
The DX-H2S-4000 displays fault conditions on the LCD screen with a flashing "FAULT"
indicator and a numeric fault code. The 4-20 mA output goes to 2 mA (below 4 mA)
to indicate a fault condition to connected systems.

4.2 Fault Code Reference Table

Code | Description                       | Likely Cause                          | Action Required
-----|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------|------------------
F01  | Sensor Open Circuit               | Sensor cell disconnected or broken    | Replace sensor cell. Refer 5.3.
F02  | Sensor Short Circuit              | Sensor cell internal fault            | Replace sensor cell. Refer 5.3.
F03  | Low Signal — Cell Depleted        | Sensor cell end-of-life              | Replace sensor cell. Cell life exceeded.
F04  | Calibration Overdue               | Cal interval exceeded (set in menu)   | Perform calibration. Refer Chapter 5.
F05  | Temperature Out of Range          | Ambient temp <-10°C or >50°C         | Check environment. If within spec, cell fault.
F06  | Memory Error                      | Internal EEPROM fault                 | Power cycle. If persistent, contact Detex support.
F07  | Signal Saturated                  | H2S > 50 ppm or inhibitory gas present | Ventilate area. Check for inhibitory gases.
F08  | Power Supply Undervoltage         | Supply <20 VDC                        | Check cable, power supply. Refer 3.2.
F09  | Communication Error               | Modbus/output wiring fault            | Check wiring. Refer 6.2.
F10  | Sensor Inhibited                  | Inhibitory gas detected (acetylene, etc.) | Identify and remove inhibitory gas source.

NOTE: Fault codes F01, F02, F03 require sensor cell replacement. Do NOT attempt to
operate the unit with these faults — the unit WILL NOT detect H2S reliably.

4.3 Inhibitory Gases
The electrochemical cell may be inhibited by the following gases:
- Acetylene: Even brief exposure >100 ppm can permanently inhibit the cell
- Ethylene: High concentrations (>500 ppm) may cause temporary inhibition
- High CO concentrations (>200 ppm) may cause cross-sensitivity (+0.3 ppm H2S equivalent per 100 ppm CO)

If the unit has been exposed to inhibitory gases, perform a full zero and span
calibration. If the cell does not recover within 30 minutes, replace the cell.

========================================================================
CHAPTER 5: CALIBRATION PROCEDURE
========================================================================

5.1 Calibration Frequency
- Routine calibration: Every 6 months (or as required by site regulations, whichever more frequent)
- OSD-110 (India) requires 6-monthly calibration minimum
- After any sensor cell replacement
- After any fault condition involving sensor degradation
- If unit has been exposed to H2S > 20 ppm for extended periods
- If unit has been stored or inactive for >3 months

5.2 Required Equipment
- Certified calibration gas cylinder: 5 ppm H2S in N2 (±2% accuracy, certificate required)
- Calibration gas regulator and tubing kit (Detex Part No. DX-4000-CAL-KIT)
- Flow rate: 0.5 L/min ±10%
- Certified zero gas: Pure N2 or dry clean air <0.1 ppm H2S

5.3 Calibration Steps
Step 1: ZERO CALIBRATION
  a. Connect zero gas (N2 or certified clean air) at 0.5 L/min
  b. Allow reading to stabilise (minimum 2 minutes)
  c. Press MENU → CALIBRATION → ZERO → CONFIRM
  d. Unit displays "ZERO OK" when accepted
  e. If zero drift >1 ppm, suspect cell contamination — replace cell before proceeding

Step 2: SPAN CALIBRATION
  a. Connect 5 ppm H2S calibration gas at 0.5 L/min
  b. Allow reading to stabilise (minimum 3 minutes)
  c. Press MENU → CALIBRATION → SPAN → ENTER TARGET VALUE (5.0) → CONFIRM
  d. Unit displays "SPAN OK" when accepted
  e. If unit cannot reach within ±0.5 ppm of target, cell is degraded — replace cell

Step 3: POST-CALIBRATION VERIFICATION
  a. Remove calibration gas
  b. Confirm reading returns to 0 ppm within 2 minutes
  c. Verify all alarm setpoints are still correctly set (re-enters defaults after major calibration)
  d. Log calibration in site calibration register with: date, tech name, gas cert number, result

5.4 Sensor Cell Replacement
When to replace:
- Any F01, F02, F03 fault code
- Calibration cannot achieve correct span (>±0.5 ppm error on 5 ppm gas)
- Cell age > 36 months
- Post-incident cell verification fails

Replacement procedure:
  a. De-energise unit (lock out power supply)
  b. Remove weatherproof housing (4 × M4 screws)
  c. Disconnect sensor cell connector (3-pin JST, gently pull straight out)
  d. Remove cell retaining clip (slide towards front)
  e. Withdraw old cell straight outward — avoid lateral force
  f. IMPORTANT: Record old cell serial number and disposal date in site records
  g. Insert new cell (Detex Part No. DX-4000-CELL-H2S) — ensure orientation marking aligns
  h. Reconnect cell connector
  i. Reassemble housing — torque screws to 1.5 Nm
  j. Energise unit — perform full calibration per Section 5.3

Compatible replacement cells: DX-4000-CELL-H2S only.
Use of non-approved cells voids warranty and may produce inaccurate readings.
Warning: H2S sensor cells contain a small quantity of dilute sulphuric acid. Handle
with gloves. Dispose as hazardous waste per local regulations.

========================================================================
CHAPTER 7: STORAGE AND TRANSPORT
========================================================================

7.1 Storage
If the unit will be inactive for more than 1 month:
- Store sensor cell in factory-sealed packaging in a cool (10–25°C), dry environment
- Do not store near volatile chemicals, solvents, or strong acids/bases
- Activate unit and run full calibration before returning to service

7.2 Prolonged Inactivity
If the unit has been inactive (powered down) for more than 3 months:
- The electrochemical cell may have degraded due to electrolyte dry-out
- Perform full calibration — if span cannot be achieved, replace cell
- Do NOT assume the unit is reading correctly without calibration after storage
