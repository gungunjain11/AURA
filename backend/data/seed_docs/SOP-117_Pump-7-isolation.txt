Title: Safe Isolation and Maintenance Procedure for Pump-7 (Hot Metal Transfer Pump)
Revision: 4
Date: 2025-03-10
Approved By: Chief Safety Officer, Plant Engineering Division
Department: Mechanical Maintenance — Hot Metal Shop

========================================================================
1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE
========================================================================
This procedure covers the complete safe isolation, maintenance, and recommissioning
of Pump-7, the primary hot metal transfer pump in the Hot Metal Shop (HMS). Pump-7
operates at temperatures up to 1450°C and pressures of 12 bar. Any failure of
isolation procedure could result in fatal injury, catastrophic equipment damage,
or uncontrolled hot metal spillage.

This SOP is mandatory for all maintenance, inspection, or repair activities on Pump-7.
No work shall begin without a valid Permit to Work (PTW) issued per SOP-089.

3. PERSONNEL AND COMPETENCY REQUIREMENTS
========================================================================
- Lead Maintenance Engineer: Minimum 5 years experience, NEBOSH IGC certified
- Safety Observer: Trained in hot metal hazards, authorised by HSE department
- Isolation Technician: Certified in LOTO (Lockout/Tagout) procedures
- Minimum team size: 3 persons at all times during isolation activity

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Mandatory:
- Full-face shield (heat-rated, min. 1600°C flash protection)
- Aluminised proximity suit
- Heat-resistant gloves (Level D — min. 500°C contact)
- Safety boots (steel-toe, chemical-resistant)
- Hard hat with chin strap
- H2S personal gas detector (calibrated within 30 days)

========================================================================
4. PRE-WORK CHECKLIST — MUST BE COMPLETED BEFORE ANY ISOLATION
========================================================================
Step 1: Obtain PTW
  1.1 Complete PTW application form (Form HSE-PTW-001)
  1.2 Identify all energy sources: electrical (11kV), hydraulic, thermal, pneumatic
  1.3 PTW must be signed by: Permit Issuer (Area Supervisor), Permit Receiver (Lead Eng.), 
      and Safety Officer
  1.4 PTW validity: Maximum 8 hours. Must be renewed if work continues beyond validity.
  1.5 Display PTW at worksite and at pump control panel

Step 2: Communication
  2.1 Notify Hot Metal Shop Control Room (Ext: 4421) of planned maintenance
  2.2 Notify adjacent work areas (HMS-B, Torpedo Car Bay) of isolation
  2.3 Confirm with Blast Furnace 2 cast house that no cast is scheduled for next 6 hours

Step 3: Atmospheric Testing
  3.1 Test for H2S: Must be < 1 ppm (TLV) before entry
  3.2 Test for CO: Must be < 25 ppm before entry
  3.3 Test for O2: Must be 19.5–23.5% before entry
  3.4 Record readings in Form HSE-ATMOS-003
  3.5 Continuous gas monitoring required during all work — use fixed detector + personal monitor

========================================================================
5. ISOLATION PROCEDURE — STEP BY STEP
========================================================================
Step 4: Electrical Isolation (LOTO)
  4.1 Coordinate with Electrical Department (Shift Electrician, contact: Ext. 4312)
  4.2 De-energise Pump-7 motor at Motor Control Centre (MCC) panel HMS-MCC-07
  4.3 Lock MCC panel with personal padlock — Lead Engineer holds the key
  4.4 Apply red LOTO tag: "DANGER — DO NOT OPERATE — Maintenance in Progress"
  4.5 Apply secondary lock by Safety Observer (each person working on equipment 
      must apply their own lock — one lock per person rule)
  4.6 PROVE DEAD: Use approved voltage tester to confirm zero energy at motor terminals
  4.7 Record in LOTO Register (Form ELE-LOTO-001)

Step 5: Hydraulic and Pneumatic Isolation
  5.1 Close hydraulic supply valve HV-117-A (located at pump base, east side)
  5.2 Close hydraulic return valve HV-117-B
  5.3 Bleed hydraulic pressure via drain valve DV-117 — confirm pressure gauge reads ZERO
  5.4 Close pneumatic supply valve PV-117-C at instrument air header
  5.5 Vent pneumatic lines — confirm gauge reads ZERO
  5.6 Apply isolation padlocks on all hand-operated valves HV-117-A, HV-117-B, PV-117-C

Step 6: Thermal and Process Isolation
  6.1 Ensure Pump-7 has been idle for minimum 4 hours (thermal cool-down)
  6.2 Verify pump casing temperature < 80°C using infrared thermometer
  6.3 Close process isolation valves IV-117-IN (inlet) and IV-117-OUT (outlet)
  6.4 Install blank flanges on both inlet and outlet pipework (use Class 900 flanges)
  6.5 Record blank flange installation in Isolation Certificate (Form PROC-ISO-002)

Step 7: Gravitational Energy Control
  7.1 Install mechanical prop/support under pump body before any disassembly
  7.2 Ensure no overhead loads (crane, suspended equipment) above work area
  7.3 Barricade zone: 5-metre radius with red/white barrier tape

========================================================================
6. MAINTENANCE ACTIVITIES PERMITTED UNDER THIS SOP
========================================================================
6.1 Bearing replacement (bearings: SKF 6316-2RS, refer OEM Manual VZ-OEM-P7-2023)
6.2 Mechanical seal replacement (seal type: John Crane Type 21, high-temp variant)
6.3 Impeller inspection and re-balancing
6.4 Pump casing inspection for erosion/corrosion (UT thickness testing required)
6.5 Motor coupling alignment check and adjustment
6.6 Instrumentation calibration (flow meter FT-117, temperature transmitter TT-117)

NOT PERMITTED under this SOP:
- Hot cutting or welding on pump body (requires separate Hot Work Permit, Form HSE-HWP-001)
- Replacement of pump casing (requires Piping and Vessel SOP-204)
- Electrical wiring modifications (requires Electrical SOP-156)

========================================================================
7. RECOMMISSIONING AFTER MAINTENANCE
========================================================================
Step 8: Restoration of Isolation
  8.1 Remove all blank flanges — confirm ALL flanges accounted for in Isolation Certificate
  8.2 Remove all locks and tags — each person removes their own lock
  8.3 Lead Engineer confirms all persons are clear of equipment
  8.4 Restore isolation valves IV-117-IN and IV-117-OUT
  8.5 Restore hydraulic and pneumatic supplies
  8.6 Restore electrical supply — remove LOTO in reverse order of application
  8.7 Electrical Dept confirms restoration in LOTO Register

Step 9: Pre-start Checks
  9.1 Lubrication: Pump and motor bearings must be lubricated (refer OEM manual VZ-OEM-P7-2023, 
      Section 4.2 — Shell Gadus S2 grease, 80g per bearing)
  9.2 Confirm coupling guard reinstalled and secured
  9.3 Confirm all instrumentation connections restored and calibrated
  9.4 Perform 30-second jog test — observe for abnormal vibration, noise, or leaks
  9.5 Start pump under load supervision for minimum 15 minutes before handing over

Step 10: PTW Closure
  10.1 Sign off PTW as COMPLETE — Permit Receiver and Permit Issuer
  10.2 File signed PTW in HSE department records (retained 5 years per Factory Act)
  10.3 Update equipment maintenance register (Form MAINT-REG-2025)

========================================================================
8. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
========================================================================
8.1 Hot metal spill: EVACUATE immediately via west exit (Emergency Assembly Point EAP-3)
    Contact: Emergency Response Team (Ext. 4444, or radio channel 7)
8.2 H2S alarm: Evacuate immediately, do not re-enter without SCBA
8.3 Electrical contact: Do NOT touch. Isolate supply at MCC. Call 4444.
8.4 Fire: Activate nearest fire alarm. Evacuate. Do not use water on hot metal.
    Use dry sand (Sand Box SB-117 located north of pump station)
