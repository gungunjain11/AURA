OSD-110: OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY STANDARD FOR METALLURGICAL INDUSTRIES
Edition: 2022 (Third Revision)
Reference: DGFASLI/OSD/MET/2022/110
Effective Date: 2022-04-01

This standard applies to all integrated steel plants, electric arc furnace operations,
coke ovens, blast furnaces, and associated metallurgical facilities operating in India.

========================================================================
CHAPTER 1: GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
========================================================================

Clause 1.1 — Scope
This standard establishes minimum occupational safety requirements for all work activities
in metallurgical industries including iron and steel manufacturing, coke production,
rolling mills, and downstream processing facilities.

Clause 1.2 — Legal Basis
This standard is issued under authority of Section 7A of the Factories Act 1948 and
is mandatory for all factories defined under Schedule I of the said Act where
metallurgical processes are carried out.

Clause 1.3 — Definitions
"Hazardous Zone": Any area where concentration of toxic gases (H2S, CO, SO2, NO2)
may exceed 50% of TLV-TWA, or where temperature exceeds 150°C, or where pressure
exceeds 5 bar above atmospheric.

"Competent Person": A person certified by the National Board for Boilers and Pressure
Vessels or equivalent recognised body to perform inspection or maintenance activities
on pressure equipment, gas systems, or electrical systems above 1000V.

"Hot Work": Any activity involving open flame, spark-producing operations, grinding,
welding, cutting, or heating that could ignite flammable or explosive materials.

========================================================================
CHAPTER 3: PERMIT TO WORK SYSTEM
========================================================================

Clause 3.1 — Mandatory PTW Activities
A valid Permit to Work (PTW) must be obtained BEFORE commencement of:
(a) Any maintenance or inspection activity on equipment handling molten metal
(b) Entry into any confined space (as defined in Clause 1.3)
(c) Hot work in any area where flammable or toxic gases may be present
(d) Electrical work on systems above 1000V
(e) Work at heights exceeding 2 metres
(f) Excavation exceeding 1.5 metres depth

Clause 3.2 — PTW Issuance Authority
PTWs may only be issued by a "Permit Issuer" who:
(a) Holds current certification in hazard identification and risk assessment
(b) Has been designated in writing by the Factory Manager
(c) Is physically present at the worksite before signing the permit

Clause 3.3 — PTW Duration and Renewal
(a) Maximum PTW duration: 8 hours for hot work, 12 hours for cold work
(b) PTW must be renewed if work continues beyond expiry — there is NO grace period
(c) Work must STOP immediately upon PTW expiry if renewal is not obtained
(d) PTW renewal requires new atmospheric testing, condition verification, and fresh signatures

Clause 3.4 — PTW Documentation
(a) All PTWs must be documented in a PTW Register maintained at the HSE office
(b) PTW records must be retained for minimum 5 years
(c) The PTW register must be reviewed by the Factory Safety Officer weekly
(d) Monthly summary reports of PTW violations must be submitted to the Factory Inspector

Clause 3.5 — PTW Cancellation
Any Competent Person or Safety Officer may cancel a PTW immediately if:
(a) Unsafe conditions develop during work
(b) The scope of work changes without re-assessment
(c) PPE is found to be inadequate or not in use

CHAPTER 6: GAS DETECTION AND MONITORING
========================================================================

Clause 6.1 — Fixed Gas Detection Systems
(a) Fixed continuous gas detectors must be installed in all areas where toxic or
    flammable gases may accumulate, including coke ovens, blast furnace cast houses,
    hot metal shops, and battery charging areas.
(b) Required detectors: H2S (alarm at 1 ppm, evacuation at 5 ppm),
    CO (alarm at 25 ppm, evacuation at 50 ppm),
    Flammable gas (alarm at 10% LEL, evacuation at 20% LEL)
(c) Fixed detectors must be calibrated at intervals not exceeding 6 months using
    certified calibration gas. Calibration records must be maintained.
(d) Defective fixed detectors must be repaired or replaced within 48 hours.
    During the downtime period, portable detectors must be deployed as substitute
    and the area must be monitored continuously by a designated observer.

Clause 6.2 — Portable Gas Detection
(a) All personnel entering hazardous zones must carry a personal gas detector
    calibrated within 30 days and bump-tested on day of use.
(b) Portable detectors must be maintained in accordance with the manufacturer's
    specifications and calibrated using certified reference gas.

Clause 6.3 — Hot Work in Gas-Hazard Areas
(a) No hot work permit shall be issued in an area where fixed gas detection is offline
    UNLESS ALL of the following conditions are simultaneously satisfied:
    (i)  A continuous personal gas monitoring system is worn by all workers
    (ii) An independent Safety Observer is stationed with written authority to stop work
    (iii) Atmospheric testing is conducted at intervals not exceeding 30 minutes
    (iv) Permit Issuer documents in the PTW the reason for fixed detector unavailability
         and the compensating measures in place
(b) If H2S readings exceed 0.8 ppm (80% of TLV) at any point during hot work,
    work must be suspended until readings return to below 0.5 ppm.

CHAPTER 7: LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) REQUIREMENTS
========================================================================

Clause 7.1 — LOTO Mandatory Scope
LOTO must be applied to control ALL energy sources before any maintenance:
(a) Electrical energy — all voltages
(b) Hydraulic energy — all pressures
(c) Pneumatic energy — all pressures
(d) Thermal energy — steam, hot fluids, hot surfaces >60°C
(e) Gravitational energy — suspended loads, stored potential energy
(f) Chemical energy — process fluids under pressure

Clause 7.2 — LOTO Procedure Requirements
(a) Each worker must apply their own personal padlock to the energy isolation point
    (one lock per person principle — multiple workers = multiple locks)
(b) Group LOTO boxes may be used for complex isolation with multiple energy sources
(c) The person who applies the lock is the ONLY person who may remove it
(d) LOTO tags must be red with legible text: "DANGER - DO NOT OPERATE"
(e) Tags must include: person's name, date/time of application, work description

Clause 7.3 — LOTO Verification
Before work begins, the isolating person must:
(a) Attempt to start the equipment to verify it will not energise
(b) Test voltage at electrical isolation points with calibrated voltage tester
(c) Attempt to operate all isolated valves to verify they cannot be operated
(d) Record verification in LOTO Register with signature

========================================================================
CHAPTER 8: PPE REQUIREMENTS FOR METALLURGICAL OPERATIONS
========================================================================

Clause 8.1 — Minimum PPE for Hot Metal Areas
All persons entering areas where hot metal, slag, or liquid steel may be present:
(a) Aluminised or metallised proximity suit (minimum specification: 1600°C radiant heat)
(b) Heat-resistant gloves, Category III, minimum 500°C contact resistance
(c) Full-face shield, heat-rated, minimum EN 166 F rated with sideshields
(d) Safety boots: steel toe, chemical and heat resistant, slip-resistant sole
(e) Hard hat with chin strap and full brim

Clause 8.2 — PPE for Confined Space Entry
(a) Full body harness with connection point for retrieval line
(b) SCBA (self-contained breathing apparatus) where O2 may be deficient or
    toxic gas levels may exceed IDLH
(c) Rescue tripod and winch stationed at entry point
(d) Communication device (intrinsically safe)

Clause 8.3 — PPE Inspection and Maintenance
(a) All PPE must be inspected before each use
(b) Heat-resistant clothing must be inspected quarterly for damage, worn areas, or
    loss of heat-reflective properties
(c) PPE that fails inspection must be removed from service immediately
(d) PPE records (issue, inspection, replacement) must be maintained per worker

========================================================================
CHAPTER 10: INCIDENT REPORTING AND INVESTIGATION
========================================================================

Clause 10.1 — Mandatory Reporting
(a) All incidents (including near-misses) must be reported within 2 hours to the
    Factory Safety Officer using Form RINL-HSE-IR-001
(b) Fatal and serious injuries must be reported to the Chief Inspector of Factories
    within 24 hours (per Section 88 of Factories Act 1948)

Clause 10.2 — Incident Investigation
(a) A formal Root Cause Analysis (RCA) must be completed for:
    - All incidents resulting in lost time (>1 day)
    - All incidents with potential for serious injury
    - All near-miss incidents in hazardous zones
(b) RCA must use the 5-Why methodology as minimum; Fault Tree Analysis for serious incidents
(c) RCA report must include corrective actions with owners and deadlines
(d) RCA reports must be reviewed at monthly Safety Committee meeting

Clause 10.3 — Learning and Sharing
(a) Safety alerts must be issued for significant incidents within 5 working days
(b) Lessons learned must be incorporated into relevant SOPs within 30 days
(c) Training refresher for affected work groups must be conducted within 15 days

========================================================================
CHAPTER 12: MAINTENANCE MANAGEMENT
========================================================================

Clause 12.1 — Planned Maintenance
(a) All pressure vessels, heat exchangers, and rotating machinery must have a documented
    maintenance schedule based on manufacturer recommendations and risk assessment
(b) Overdue maintenance items must be escalated to the Plant Manager within 7 days
(c) Safety-critical equipment (pressure relief valves, gas detectors, fire suppression,
    emergency shutdown systems) must NOT operate beyond their scheduled maintenance date

Clause 12.2 — Pressure Relief Valve (PRV) Testing
(a) All PRVs must be bench-tested at intervals not exceeding 12 months, or as per
    Indian Boiler Regulations, whichever is more stringent
(b) PRVs on high-pressure systems (>15 bar) must be tested every 6 months
(c) PRV test records must be maintained and available for inspection

Clause 12.3 — Equipment Inspection Records
(a) A complete inspection and maintenance history must be maintained for each piece
    of safety-critical equipment
(b) Records must be accessible to Factory Inspector on demand
(c) Records must be retained for the life of the equipment plus 5 years
