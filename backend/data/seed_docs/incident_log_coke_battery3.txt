INCIDENT REPORT — IR-2025-0089
========================================================================
Date: 2025-02-14
Time: 14:23
Location: Coke Battery 3, Oven No. 22 (Pushing Platform)
Type: Near-Miss — Potential H2S Exposure
Severity Rating: HIGH
Reported By: Shift Safety Officer T. Krishnan
Persons Involved: 3 workers (pushing team, names in confidential annex)

Description:
During a scheduled pushing operation on Coke Battery 3 Oven No. 22, an oven door
failed to seal properly due to degraded door jamb refractories. Coke oven gas
(containing H2S, CO, benzene, and light hydrocarbons) was observed leaking from
the oven door periphery.

The area's fixed H2S detector (CB3-GD-04) was offline at this time — it had been
sent for scheduled calibration 3 days prior and had not yet been returned to service.
The pushing team was wearing personal H2S detectors; two of the three workers'
personal detectors alarmed at readings of 2.1 ppm and 1.9 ppm H2S respectively.
The third worker's detector did not alarm. Workers evacuated the area immediately
following detector alarms.

Post-incident atmospheric testing showed peak H2S of 3.8 ppm in the oven door
leakage zone — well below IDLH (100 ppm) but significantly above TLV (1 ppm).

Workers were medically assessed — no symptoms of H2S exposure noted. All three
returned to work same shift.

Root Cause (Initial Assessment):
1. Oven door refractories degraded due to missed quarterly inspection (last inspection
   was 7 months prior, against a 3-month schedule)
2. Fixed H2S detector CB3-GD-04 offline simultaneously with hazardous operation
3. No compensating measures in place for detector downtime (as required by OSD-110 Clause 6.1(d))
4. Third worker's personal detector had not been bump-tested on day of use

Corrective Actions Raised:
CA-001: Replace oven door refractories on Oven 22 — DUE: 2025-02-21 — Owner: Mechanical Dept
CA-002: Implement monthly (not quarterly) oven door inspection schedule — DUE: 2025-03-01 — Owner: Maintenance Planner
CA-003: Require documented daily bump test for all personal gas detectors in CO Plant — DUE: 2025-02-20 — Owner: Safety Officer
CA-004: Define compensating measures SOP for fixed detector downtime — DUE: 2025-03-15 — Owner: HSE Dept
CA-005: Repair or replace CB3-GD-04 within 48 hours of calibration completion — DUE: 2025-02-17 — Owner: Instrumentation Dept

Status: CLOSED — All corrective actions verified complete as of 2025-04-01.

INCIDENT REPORT — IR-2025-0112
========================================================================
Date: 2025-03-08
Time: 09:45
Location: Coke Battery 1, Coal Tower Conveyor CV-CO-01
Type: Near-Miss — Mechanical (caught-in hazard)
Severity Rating: MEDIUM
Reported By: Maintenance Supervisor A. Kulkarni
Persons Involved: 1 worker (name in confidential annex)

Description:
A maintenance technician was clearing a coal spillage near the tail pulley of
conveyor CV-CO-01 when the conveyor was inadvertently restarted by a second
worker in the control cabin who was unaware of the maintenance activity.

The technician was not wearing the required LOTO padlock — he had not formally
isolated the conveyor before beginning the cleaning task, assuming the short
duration (<5 min) of the task did not require LOTO. The technician withdrew
his hand from near the tail pulley immediately upon hearing the conveyor start.

No injury occurred. However, the contact zone between the technician's hand and
the tail pulley was less than 20 cm at restart.

Root Cause:
1. LOTO not applied for a maintenance task of any duration — direct violation of OSD-110 Clause 7.1
2. Inadequate task planning — worker self-assessed task as "too short for LOTO"
3. No communication between maintenance technician and control cabin operator
4. Supervisor was not informed of the task initiation

Corrective Actions:
CA-001: Mandatory LOTO refresher training for all Coke Oven maintenance staff — COMPLETED 2025-03-22
CA-002: Communication protocol: any conveyor work requires notification to control cabin — COMPLETED 2025-03-15
CA-003: "Zero energy" safety culture initiative launched across Coke Oven plant — IN PROGRESS

Status: OPEN — CA-003 safety initiative ongoing.

INCIDENT REPORT — IR-2025-0178
========================================================================
Date: 2025-06-17
Time: 21:15
Location: Coke Battery 3, Gas Offtake Section
Type: Near-Miss — Gas Detection Failure During Night Shift
Severity Rating: CRITICAL
Reported By: Night Shift Safety Officer M. Pillai
Persons Involved: 5 workers (pushing team + 1 contract worker)

Description:
During night-shift pushing operations on Coke Battery 3, a contract worker
reported smelling a strong odour (rotten egg) in the vicinity of the gas offtake
mainline, suggesting H2S presence. The olfactory alert was reported to the shift
supervisor at approximately 21:10.

Investigation revealed:
1. Fixed H2S detector CB3-GD-04 was OFFLINE (had been offline since 2025-06-14 —
   3 days at time of incident). The detector's LCD display was showing "FAULT" state.
2. The fault condition of CB3-GD-04 had not been entered into the maintenance
   management system (BPSys) until 2025-06-17 17:00 — 3 days after the fault occurred.
3. No compensating measures (portable detector, safety observer) had been formally
   arranged per OSD-110 Clause 6.1(d) and CA-004 from IR-2025-0089.
4. The contract worker was not carrying a personal H2S detector (personal detector
   assigned to him was found in the site office locker, uncharged).
5. Night shift supervisor K. Anand was unaware that CB3-GD-04 was offline.

Portable atmospheric testing conducted at 21:25 showed H2S = 1.4 ppm at gas offtake
mainline isolation valve (above TLV of 1 ppm). Source identified as a minor seal
leak on isolation valve IV-CO3-GOT-07.

All workers evacuated. Seal leak repaired by 23:45. Atmospheric testing confirmed
H2S < 0.2 ppm before return to work.

Root Cause (Initial Assessment):
Primary Cause: Fixed H2S gas detector CB3-GD-04 failure not managed per corrective
action CA-004 from IR-2025-0089 (same root cause recurred despite prior corrective action).

Contributing Factors:
1. CB3-GD-04 fault not entered into BPSys for 3 days — delay in notification to safety team and shift supervisors
2. Corrective Action CA-004 (compensating measures SOP) created on paper but not effectively communicated to night shift supervisors
3. Contract worker PPE compliance not verified by shift supervisor
4. Minor valve seal leak on IV-CO3-GOT-07 not identified in last maintenance walkthrough (2025-06-10)
5. Night shift handover process did not include mention of offline detector status

Immediate Corrective Actions (Night of Incident):
IA-001: CB3-GD-04 fault ticket escalated to Priority 1 — Instrumentation team to respond within 24 hours
IA-002: Contract worker management agency notified — PPE compliance audit for all contract workers
IA-003: Night shift supervisor KPI review for equipment status handover

Formal Corrective Actions (Raised 2025-06-18):
FCA-001: Mandatory BPSys entry within 4 hours of any safety-critical equipment fault — DUE: 2025-07-01 — Owner: Maintenance Manager
FCA-002: Automated SMS/email alert to safety team and shift supervisors when CB3-GD-04 (or equivalent) goes offline — DUE: 2025-07-15 — Owner: IT/Instrumentation
FCA-003: Update shift handover checklist to include equipment status for all gas detectors — DUE: 2025-06-25 — Owner: Production Manager CO
FCA-004: Retraining of all shift supervisors on compensating measures SOP (procedure from CA-004) — DUE: 2025-06-30 — Owner: HSE Dept
FCA-005: Mandatory pre-shift PPE inspection for all contract workers — DUE: 2025-06-25 — Owner: Contract Manager
FCA-006: Replace CB3-GD-04 immediately (unit is beyond economic repair) — WO: WO-2025-1892 — Priority: CRITICAL — DUE: 2025-06-20

Status as of 2025-06-22: OPEN
CB3-GD-04: STILL OFFLINE — WO-2025-1892 replacement unit on order, ETA 2025-06-25
FCA-001 through FCA-005: In progress

NOTE: PTW-2025-0461 (hot work on Coke Battery 3, issued 2025-06-22) was issued while
CB3-GD-04 remains offline. Safety Officer P. Naidu raised objection — overridden by
Area Supervisor. Refer PTW register entry for details.

INCIDENT REPORT — IR-2025-0134
========================================================================
Date: 2025-04-22
Time: 11:30
Location: Coke Battery 3, Coke Side Quench Car Track
Type: First Aid Injury — Burn
Severity Rating: LOW (recordable)
Reported By: Shift Safety Officer T. Krishnan
Persons Involved: 1 worker

Description:
A quench car operator sustained a minor burn (approx. 3 cm² area, second degree)
to the back of his right hand when steam from the quench car nozzle made unexpected
contact during positioning operation. The operator was wearing heat-resistant gloves
that had a small tear in the knuckle area, which had not been detected during pre-shift
inspection.

Treatment: First aid at plant medical centre. No lost time.

Root Cause: Damaged PPE continued in use; inspection checklist for PPE not signed off
that morning (operator acknowledged signing without checking).

Corrective Action: PPE inspection reinforcement training, new PPE issued.
Status: CLOSED 2025-05-01.
